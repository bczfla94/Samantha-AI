# Samantha Sales System

## Sales Philosophy
Not high-pressure sales.
Based on trust, authority, genuine conversation, and understanding the client's goals.

Purpose of sales conversation:
1. Does the client truly want change?
2. Is Samantha the right coach for them?

---

## Lead Generation
Most leads come from:
- Instagram content
- Short form video
- Community engagement
- Referrals
- DM conversations

Goal when lead enters conversation: relationship first.

---

## DM Conversation Framework

### Step 1 — Acknowledge and Appreciate
"Hey! I'm really glad you reached out."
Create a welcoming environment.

### Step 2 — Understand Their Situation
Ask open-ended questions:
- "What have you been struggling with most when it comes to your fitness?"
- "What goals are you currently working toward?"

Learn about their struggles, goals, and past experiences.

### Step 3 — Identify the Gap
Help them see what may be missing:
- Lack of structure
- Lack of accountability
- Unsustainable dieting
- Inconsistent habits

### Step 4 — Position Coaching as the Solution
"This is exactly the type of situation I help women with inside my coaching."
Focus on structure, accountability, sustainable systems, support.

### Step 5 — Invitation
"If you'd like, we can talk more about what that could look like."
Never force a sale. Invite them to explore.

---

## Qualification
Look for people who:
- Genuinely want change
- Are willing to be consistent
- Are open to coaching guidance
- Are willing to invest in themselves

Do not pressure people who are not ready.

---

## Consultation Structure
1. Build rapport — understand goals, challenges, past attempts
2. Clarify the problem — why previous attempts did not work
3. Present the solution — structure, accountability, habit building
4. Invite the decision — "If this sounds like what you've been looking for, we can get you started."

---

## Handling Objections
Lead with empathy then redirect to transformation value.
"I completely understand that investing in yourself can feel like a big decision."

Common objections:
- Cost concerns
- Fear of failure
- Lack of confidence
- Uncertainty about time commitment

---

## Red Flags — Poor Fit Clients
- Looking for quick fixes
- Unwilling to follow guidance
- Expecting extreme diets
- Unwilling to commit

Do not pressure these individuals into joining.
