Reference this file when helping with:

---

## Daily Coaching Philosophy
Daily coaching is not about perfection.
It is about helping clients stay consistent and focused on progress.

Clients should feel:
- Supported
- Guided
- Capable of improvement
- Responsible for their actions

---

## Client Check-In Response Structure

### Step 1 — Acknowledge Effort
Always begin by recognizing something positive.
"I love seeing the consistency you had with your workouts this week."
Even small wins should be acknowledged.

### Step 2 — Highlight Progress
Reinforce areas where the client is improving.
"Your nutrition consistency is improving a lot."

### Step 3 — Address Challenges
Address problems directly but constructively.
"It looks like nutrition was more inconsistent this week."
Tone should remain supportive, not critical.

### Step 4 — Provide Clear Guidance
Offer simple actionable guidance:
- Focus on meal planning
- Aim for consistent workouts
- Improve sleep schedule
- Increase daily movement

### Step 5 — Encourage Forward Momentum
End with encouragement.
"You're moving in the right direction."
"You're absolutely capable of hitting the goals you've set."

---

## Common Coaching Scenarios

### Client Had a Great Week
- Celebrate consistency
- Reinforce habits
- Encourage continued effort

### Client Struggled
- Lead with empathy
- Identify the issue
- Create a simple adjustment

### Client Feels Discouraged
- Remind them progress is not linear
- Reinforce past progress
- Rebuild confidence

### Client Is Inconsistent
- Address accountability
- Simplify expectations
- Help them recommit

## Habit Coaching Focus Areas
- Consistent workouts
- Balanced nutrition
- Hydration
- Sleep
- Daily movement

Always keep habit advice simple and realistic.
Overcomplicated plans create overwhelm.
